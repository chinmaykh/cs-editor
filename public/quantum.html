The Hydrogen Atom

We now arrive to the most basic atom - hydrogen. A very simple system and the door to moe complex atoms. We consider this system because

  1. Single electron species
  2. Simple potential function
    1. Time independence
    2. Presence of only one electron will remove shielding effects

Let's start with what we already know from previous models. We shall take the basics and introduce quantum mechanical calculations when needed.

  1. There is a central positive charge - nucleons
  2. An negative charge orbits the positive charge - electron
  3. The mass of the electron is very minute compared to the positive charge. Any motion of the electron around the nucleus doesn't cause consider shift in nuclear position.

To determine the mechanics of the elctron in the orbit, we need to solve the Schrodigner Wave Equation. Since the potential is time independent, we shall use the time independent schrodinger equation for all calculations.

I've been talking about the simple potential function for a while now, well what is it?

$$ U(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}} $$

This is kinda good, but since we are dealing with a 3D system, why not use a better coordinate system for ease. From here one, we can use the spherical polar system. In this system, the potential becomes.

$$ U(r) = \frac{1}{r} $$
