Infinite Potential Well

We start with the easiest potential which exists. The infinite potential well. Experience from the free particle shows us that we cannot make a simpler case. In the case of a potential well, we have the properties.

  1. The well has two boundaries. Each having the infinite potential till infinity
  2. Between the two wells, the potential is zero.

In both the particle and the wave model, the entity gets reflected at the boundary. It cannot penetarte the barrier. So how does it localize itself inside the well ?

We have a differential equation and we need to solve it. We use the constraints of the system as the boundary conditions to solve the differential equation.

A simple sinusoid is the answer. Well, actually it's not just the answer. Sinusoids (plural) are the answers. There are multiple sinusoids which satisfy the Schrodinger wave equation for this system. Which is the correct answer ?

All of them. We need to take a little bit of all of them to construct our solution. The easiest way to see how much to take of each is by normalizing the wave function. By adding one other constarint, all the weights get fixed.
